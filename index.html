Who I Am

I have been a Professional Engineer (Electrical) registered with the Association of Professional Engineers and Geoscientists of Alberta for over 25 years (APEGA). I have a long term association of 30+ years with the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE) through active memberships with the Circuits and Systems Society (CAS), Communications Society (COMSOC), Computer Society (CS), and the Signal Processing Society (SP). For over 2 decades I have worked on embedded product development for a wide range of resource, industrial and governmental clientele throughout the world.


WHAT I DO

My professional career began in the Canadian Arctic in the late 1980s and has seen me to five continents. I've worked in -50C to +50C temperature extremes; from ice-cap to jungle and from desert to ocean.

I've helped develop terrestrial (Sercel SYLEDIS) and satellite (GPS/GNSS) positioning systems, advanced digital wavelet audio processing for communication in extreme environments (DXI), remote wireless sensors for marine and Arctic deployment using meteor-burst communication (MBC) and low-earth orbit (LEO) satellite channels, real-time signal processors for synthetic aperature RADAR (SAR), ice mapping of the North-West Passage using side-looking airborne RADAR (SLAR), wireless internet based SCADA systems (ArcSCADA), low-cost embedded processoring for the Internet-of-Things (IoT), and more.

I started working with Linux and UNIX-like systems (BSD, QNX) some 25 years ago and will continue to do so until something better comes along. Currently, I do most of my development using ARM processors.
